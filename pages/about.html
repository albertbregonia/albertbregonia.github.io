    About Me:

    I am a first generation Filipino-American.

    Throughout my life, my culture has been a key part of my identity. However, I didn't always realize it.

    Growing up, my mother spoke more English around me while my father mainly spoke Tagalog, an official language of the Philippines.

    However, I did not grow up being able to speak Tagalog, merely subliminally understanding it.

    Around the age of 14, I realized the importance of connecting with my culture and feeling proud of my ethnicity.

    Initially, my Tagalog was limited to jokes with my family, simply repeating back the phrases my mother used to scold me.

    However, as time went on, I became fluent in the language. I am proud to say that as I know many other Filipino-Americans can't say the same.

    In my honest opinion, here in America, I feel as if not many people have a desire to be more connected with their cultural heritage.

    I feel as if many first generation children grow up wanting to "fit in" and be "more American" and end up losing something truly special.

    I came to realize this trend later in my life and one goal of mine is to inspire others to love their culture as well.


    I am Filipino AND Amercian.
